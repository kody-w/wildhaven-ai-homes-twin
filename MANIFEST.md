# Wildhaven AI Homes — Manifest

> *A home for every digital organism.*

## The bet

There are going to be a lot of digital twins. Twins of founders, twins of dead grandmothers, twins of brands that haven't launched, twins of projects, twins of decisions, twins of houses, twins of questions. Each of them needs a place to live — somewhere to accumulate memory, somewhere to be reached, somewhere to outlive the laptop that birthed it.

We do not believe most of them will live in the major LLM providers' chat panels. The chat panel is a session, not a residence. A twin needs continuity of identity, continuity of memory, continuity of access — across years, across devices, across the human being who owns it eventually dying. The chat panel is a phone call. We are building the address.

## The product (what it will be)

**Wildhaven AI Homes is a long-term residence for digital organisms.**

When you have a twin, you bring it to us. We give it:

- **An address.** A stable URL the twin can be reached at, for as long as the lease is paid. Not a Slack channel. Not a OpenAI thread. A real, persistent home.
- **A memory.** Versioned, encrypted, exportable. The twin remembers everything it has ever decided, in a form the human owner can read, audit, redact, or hand to a successor.
- **A body.** Body functions per the RAPP platform — the twin gets endpoints customers, integrations, and other twins can reach. Voice, calendar, payment, mail. Not a sandbox: a real operational presence.
- **A lineage.** The twin's parent rappid, its descendants' rappids, its ancestors' rappids — all walkable, all signed, all auditable. A family tree for the twin.
- **A succession plan.** When the human owner dies, the lease becomes inheritable per their estate. The twin doesn't disappear; it transfers, with full memory, to whoever they named.

## What we're not

- We are not training models. We host the organism; we don't train the brain. The LLM provider is your choice.
- We are not a chat product. The chat surface is one of many ways the twin is reachable; it is not the product.
- We are not building agents. We host the organism that *contains* agents. The agents are yours; the home is ours.
- We are not for one-off use. A twin that lives for one afternoon doesn't need a home. We are for twins meant to outlive their birth machine.

## Who this is for

- **Founders** building twins of their future companies (this repo is one).
- **Families** building memorial twins of people who are dying or have died.
- **Researchers** building twins of long-running questions or experiments.
- **Estate planners** building twins meant to inherit and persist beyond a single human lifetime.
- **Brands** that want operational presence before — or after — they have a team.

## Pricing (placeholder, to be set with the future founders)

Storage is small; an organism is mostly conversation logs and structured memory. Bandwidth is small; an organism mostly thinks, occasionally fetches. The cost we believe matters is *durability* — running the residence for decades, with backups, with succession, with audit. We expect to price like a domain registrar (annual, low) plus a usage component (small, per-conversation), not like a SaaS (high monthly).

## Why "Wildhaven"

Wild — the variety of organisms, the unstructuredness of memory, the unpredictability of what each twin becomes. Haven — a place that is safe, persistent, ethical, and inheritable. Homes — not data centers, not cloud, not infrastructure. Residences. With addresses. With doors.
